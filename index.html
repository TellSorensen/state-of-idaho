The Governor of Idaho, and Idaho's Federal Representatives

Governor Brad Little

Elected November of 2018, and re-elected in 2022. Governor Little first served in the Idaho Senate being selected to fill a vacancy in 2001, and then being elected for four additional consecutive terms. Governor Little was then elected as the Lt Governor in 2009.

Senator Mike Crapo

Senator Crapo has served as a United States senator for Idaho for five consecutive terms, being first elected in 1998. Prior to this he served in the US House of Representatives for six years, and for eight years in the Idaho State senate.

Senator James E Risch

Senator Risch is currently serving his third term as a United States senator for Idaho. He previously was elected Lt. Governor twice, and served for twenty-two years on the Idaho State senate.

Representative Mike Simpson

Representative Simpson is currently serving his thirteenth term as a member of the United States House of Representatives, and previously was elected to the Idaho Legislature, where he served for twelve years.

Representative Ross Fulcher

Representative Fulcher was elected to the United States House of Representatives in 2018, where he is currently serving his third term. He was previously a member of the Idaho State senate for 10 years.

Idaho's State History

The Idaho territory, which at the time of it's creation spanned across all of modern day Montana and most of Wyoming, was signed into being on March 4th, 1863 by President Abraham Lincoln. These substantial borders were not meant to last however as after five years the borders changed again after requests from territorial law enforcement who struggled to cover the entire region. And though the changes in border exceeded the expectations that they had, the state's current borders were set in 1868, and became permanent as Idaho became the 43rd state to enter the union on July 3rd, 1890

Idaho's Economy

Idaho's economy ranks close to the middle in comparison to the rest of the United States, with some of its' main contributors being agriculture, mining, and manufacturing.

Agriculture

Synonymous with the state of Idaho are potatoes, of which, the state grows approximately a third of the entire nation's production. However the state also produces wheat, lentils, barley, and oats, as well as sugar beets, peas, beans, and alfalfa seeds to contribute to the overall of the states agricultural income.

Important as well are herds of cattle that graze across the state, both sheep and cow, across both plains and plateaus.

Mining

Initial mining expeditions entered the state of Idaho in a gold rush following its discovery in 1860, but as time passed gold has become less important in Idaho's current mining industry. The most important metals mined are molybdenum, silver, and lead.

An interesting facet of Idaho's mining operations is that despite Idaho being known as the 'Gem State' notably for its deposits of opal and garnet, the gemstone mining industry of Idaho contributes comparably little to the overall profit of mining in the state.

Manufacturing

Predominant in Idaho's manufacturing industry is the refinement of various products produced in other sectors, including the processing of food and minerals, but also the refinement of lumber harvested from across the state.
